## The Two Core Infant Sensory Systems

### System 1: Approximate Number System (ANS) - Quantitative Sense
**What**: Preverbal, nonsymbolic numerical capacity
**When**: Present from infancy (4+ months)
**Characteristics**:
- Represents **large, approximate numerical magnitudes**
- Ratio-dependent (Weber fraction ~0.3: can distinguish 8 vs 12 but not 8 vs 9)
- Works across modalities: visual, auditory, tactile
- Associated with **intraparietal sulcus (IPS)** in brain

**Key Discovery**: 6-month-old infants' ANS acuity **predicts mathematical ability at 3.5 years** - even controlling for IQ!

**Developmental Timeline**:
- **4 months**: Discriminate 1:4 ratio (e.g., 4 vs 16 items)
- **4-6 months**: With intersensory redundancy (audio+video), discriminate 1:3 ratio
- **6-9 months**: Distinguish continuous quantity (liquid volume)
- **11 months**: Understand ordinal relationships (which is greater)

### System 2: Precise Individual Tracking System - Qualitative Sense
**What**: Precise tracking of small numbers (subitizing)
**When**: Present from birth
**Characteristics**:
- Represents **precise tracking of distinct individuals** (up to 3-4 items)
- Performance depends on **absolute number**, not ratio
- Sensitive to continuous variables (area, contour length)
- Works across categories (objects, sounds, actions)

**Key Finding**: Infants initially use **non-numerical cues** (area, contour) even for discrete items!

## The Two Core Infant Color Systems

### System 1: Wavelength Discrimination - Early Color Sense
**What**: Basic ability to distinguish different light wavelengths
**When**: Development from birth
**Timeline**:
- **1-2 months**: Red (650nm) vs Green (545nm) discrimination
- **2-3 months**: Green vs Yellow discrimination emerges
- **3 months**: Red vs Yellow discrimination
- **By 3 months**: **FULLY TRICHROMATIC** (red-green + blue-yellow mechanisms active)

**Mechanism**:
- L-cones (red): Mature first
- M-cones (green): Mature around 2-3 months
- S-cones (blue): Mature latest (3+ months)

**The Qualia Shift**: By 3-6 months, infants extract **hue independently** from lightness and saturation

### System 2: Color Categorization - Structured Color World
**What**: Organization of continuous spectrum into discrete categories
**When**: Emerges around 4-6 months
**Evidence**:
- 4-month-olds categorize colors **perceptually**, grouping similar hues
- **Hue categories form BEFORE language learning**
- Categorical boundaries (~equal physical distances show different perceptual distances)
- Similar to adult color categories (blue, green, yellow, red)

**The Major Finding**: By **6-9 months**, the phenomenal character of color experience is **comparable to adults**!

**Advanced Discovery (7-8 months)**:
- Infants distinguish **gold from yellow** (same hue, different glossiness)
- Uses **surface specular reflectance representation** (not just wavelength)
- Shows sophisticated color constancy development

- **Number conservation** doesn't appear until age 5-7 (concrete operations)

### Modern Evidence
- **Infants have rich number sense from 4+ months**
